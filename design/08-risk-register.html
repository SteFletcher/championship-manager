R-01Match engine produces unrealistic distributions — goal/card rates outside believable bands, undermining the simulation's credibility.236Statistical realism harness over thousands of sims asserts goal-rate, shot-funnel, possession, and home-advantage bands; mismatches must dominate.2 (L1×I2)
R-02Determinism breaks on save/restore — a resumed career diverges from an uninterrupted one, breaking replays and fairness.236Single seeded RNG with serializable state; explicit save/restore roundtrip test that continues identically; engine never mutates player objects mid-match.1 (L1×I1)
R-03Match-day pause/sub edge cases — sent-off players reappearing, emergency keeper logic failing, formation remap dropping players.236Structural invariants across 500 matches: sent-off never reappear; #ensureKeeper fallback; formation remap prefers natural positions with deficit landing up front.2 (L1×I2)
R-04Save-format drift — schema changes break existing saves silently.326SAVE_VERSION constant (currently 6); restore throws on mismatch rather than loading incompatible state.2 (L1×I2)
R-05AI squad gutting — AI-to-AI transfers or AI bids hollow out squads below viability.224canRelease enforces ≥15 players and ≥2 GKs always retained; transfer activity is probabilistic and bounded.1 (L1×I1)
R-06localStorage limits / corruption — a long career's JSON exceeds quota or a corrupt blob refuses to load.224Save is a single flat JSON, well within 5 MB quota; inbox capped at 120, free agents at 40; loadGame catches parse errors and returns null.1 (L1×I1)
R-07Scouting attribute masking leaks truth — the estimate range's centre or sort order reveals the real attribute.224Range is offset by a per-player deterministic hash so the centre never equals the truth; sorting uses visibleMid, not the hidden value; range always contains but is not centred on the truth.1 (L1×I1)

R-09Season-end transition complexity — the large end-of-season sequence (development, retirements, promotion, expectations, cup reset) has a subtle ordering bug.224Multi-season headless simulation tests in game.test.js assert invariants across many seasons; the sequence is documented in the engine spec.2 (L1×I2)
R-10Performance degeneration in long careers — many seasons of history or large free-agent pools slow the loop.212Inbox and free-agent arrays are capped; history is one entry per season (tiny); season stats reset yearly.1 (L1×I1)
R-11Accessibility gaps — keyboard or screen-reader users blocked on dense tables.224Focusable team rows with Enter/Space; aria-live on commentary & squad panel; status conveyed by text as well as colour; visible focus outlines.2 (L1×I2)

4. Top Mitigations Summary

The single most effective mitigation

The engine/UI purity boundary (R-02, R-03, R-09) is the keystone: because the engine is pure and DOM-free, the entire 148-test suite can run in Node in ~2.5s and assert properties that would be impractical to verify through a browser. Most risks reduce to "the test suite catches it" precisely because that boundary exists.

Open / monitored

R-11 (a11y) and R-12 (scope) are the two risks with residual exposure that warrants ongoing attention — a11y because dense table UIs are inherently hard for assistive tech, and scope because the genre invites endless feature requests. Both are tracked in the roadmap rather than "solved".
