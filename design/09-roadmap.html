09 · Future Roadmap

Phased enhancements from the ideation backlog, ranked by impact & effort

How this was built

The roadmap is grounded in the docs/ideation/2026-07-11-next-improvement-ideation.md backlog, which scores candidate features against the current codebase. Items already shipped are marked Done; the rest are sequenced into near, mid, and long-term horizons by their impact-to-effort ratio and their dependence on existing systems.

1. Delivered (the current feature set)

These are the capabilities the reverse-engineered design documents describe as existing. They form the foundation everything else builds on.

DELIVERED in production

Resumable match engine + live match day

Minute-by-minute MatchSim with pause, substitutions, auto-pause at key moments, extra time, penalty shootouts, live ratings, and a formation pitch view.

DELIVERED in production

Two divisions with promotion/relegation + 40-club cup

22-round double round-robin, two-up/two-down, dynamic board expectations, and a knockout cup spanning all clubs with byes and rounds.

DELIVERED in production

In-match tactical management

Formation and mentality changes during any pause, with AI managers reading the game: chasing when behind, shutting up shop when ahead, digging in when short-handed.

DELIVERED in production

Scouting & attribute masking

Other clubs' attributes show as estimate ranges until a £15k scout report reveals the truth; pre-match opposition reports show form, predicted XI, and danger man.

DELIVERED in production

Fanbase-driven economy & stadium expansion

Attendance follows a results-driven fanbase, capped by the ground; board-approved capacity expansions unlock higher gates; AI boards expand too.

DELIVERED in production

Manager reputation & the job market

Sacking opens a job market at lesser clubs; strong seasons attract poach offers from bigger clubs; reputation tracks trophies, promotions, and failures.

DELIVERED 2026-07-12

Authentic 1995/96 English world data

The 40 clubs are now the real 1995/96 Premier League and Division One, and every club fields its genuine 18-man period squad (Cantona, Shearer, Le Tissier…) from squads1995.js, with attributes still generated deterministically from club tier.

DELIVERED EE-1 · 2026-07-12

Extensible match-engine core & regression armour

The minute loop is a named phase pipeline; every event carries structured data; golden-master fixtures and a statistical invariant suite pin behaviour. First milestone of the Engine Evolution track.

DELIVERED EE-2 · 2026-07-13

Ten detailed positions & formation geometry

GK/DR/DC/DL/DM/MR/MC/ML/AMC/ST with secondary positions and a familiarity ladder replacing the binary out-of-position penalty; formations are slot lists with pitch coordinates; v6 saves migrate deterministically (save v7).

DELIVERED EE-3 · 2026-07-13

Eight real attributes drive the engine

Passing, finishing, tackling, positioning, pace, stamina, plus handling and reflexes for keepers. atk/def survive as derived composites; shot resolution, penalties, and keeper duels read the specific attribute (save v8).

2. Near-Term Horizons

High-impact, lower-effort features that close loops the current systems already opened. Each of these now has a full buildable specification — requirements, engine design, data-model deltas, UI wireframes, and test plan — in 10 · Horizon 1 Specs. In parallel, the Engine Evolution track is underway: three of its seven milestones (EE-1 core, EE-2 positions, EE-3 attributes) have shipped, with EE-4 (per-player instructions) next.

HORIZON 1 · NEAR low–medium effort

Assistant manager / auto-resolve week

A "holiday" or "assistant takes charge" flow for weeks the player wants to skip — simulating the user's match and applying a default lineup. Closes the gap for players who want to fast-forward through dead rubbers.

  • Basis: the Continue flow already auto-sims when there's no user fixture; extending it to "play but don't watch" is a small step.
  • Effort: Low. Mostly a UI button + a flag to advanceWeek.
  • Risk: must keep determinism intact.
  • Spec: 10 · §2 (AST).
HORIZON 1 · NEAR low effort

League statistics pages

Dedicated screens for assists, discipline, and clean sheets across the league, extending the top-scorers table already on the Club & Board screen.

  • Basis: seasonStats already aggregates apps, goals, assists, and ratings — the data exists, only the views are missing.
  • Effort: Low. Pure UI (plus two small accumulators for discipline and clean sheets — see spec).
  • Spec: 10 · §3 (STA).
HORIZON 1 · NEAR medium effort

Player interaction & morale events

Direct player interactions — praise, fine, warn — that move morale, plus morale- driven news events (unhappy stars, transfer requests). Gives the squad screen more to do beyond list/renew.

  • Basis: morale exists and moves with results but is otherwise inert; the inbox + pending-offers pattern is the template.
  • Effort: Medium. New state + UI + news wiring.
  • Spec: 10 · §4 (INT).

3. Mid-Term Horizons

Larger systems that deepen existing loops; higher effort, back-loaded payoff.

HORIZON 2 · MID high effort

Loan system

Send players out on loan for match fitness (condition/form recovery + development acceleration for youngsters) and loan in cover for injuries. A natural extension of the transfer market that uses a time-bounded temporary move rather than a permanent sale.

  • Basis: transfers, youth intake, and development already exist; loans thread through the same #completeTransfer-style path with a return date.
  • Effort: High. New entity (loan), season-end return logic, UI.
  • Impact: gives youth players a purpose and adds a squad- management lever beyond buy/sell.
HORIZON 2 · MID medium effort

Staff & facilities

Hire assistant coaches, scouts, and physios; build training facilities and youth academies as additional money sinks that improve development rates, scouting range, and injury recovery. Generalises the stadium-expansion pattern.

  • Basis: stadium expansion proved the board-approval + multi-week-build + cost model; finances need more sinks at successful clubs.
  • Effort: Medium-High. Reusable build/quote pattern; new staff entity.
HORIZON 2 · MID medium effort

Set-piece & training tactics

Per-player training focus (improve a specific attribute slowly over weeks) and set-piece assignments (corner taker, penalty taker, free-kick specialist) that feed the match engine's chance resolution.

  • Basis: the match engine already picks shooters and providers by weight; set-piece roles are a weighted-pick override.
  • Effort: Medium. Development already mutates attributes yearly; training is a weekly micro-develop step.

4. Long-Term Horizons

Ambitious expansions that materially grow the world or the play model.

HORIZON 3 · LONG high effort

European competition

A continental cup for the top finishers — a third competition layered on the calendar, with a group stage or straight knockout and midweek slots. Explicitly deferred at build time.

  • Basis: the cup engine (cup.js) generalises to any entrant count; the calendar (season.js) already interleaves cup days.
  • Effort: High. New entrant selection, fixture congestion, UI.
  • Dependency: benefits from a richer squad (loans/staff) first.
HORIZON 3 · LONG high effort

International management

Managing a national team alongside or instead of a club, with qualifying campaigns and tournaments. The ultimate career capstone.

  • Basis: reputation and the job market already model managerial standing; an international job is a special case of acceptJob.
  • Effort: High. New selection model, tournament format, calendar coordination.
HORIZON 3 · LONG high effort

Hotseat / multiplayer career

Two or more human managers in the same world, taking turns. Requires a turn-passing protocol and careful handling of simultaneous fixtures.

  • Basis: the engine is already deterministic and turn-based; the blocker is purely the Continue-flow ownership model.
  • Effort: High. Restructures the core loop; touches every screen.
  • Rejected earlier for scope; reconsidered here as the long-term ceiling.
HORIZON 3 · LONG medium effort

Replay & match sharing

Export a match's seed + inputs as a shareable token that anyone can replay deterministically — "watch this incredible comeback" as a URL. A direct payoff of the determinism contract.

  • Basis: MatchSim already replays identically from a seed; a share is just a serialised setup + seed.
  • Effort: Medium. Serialization + a replay-only UI mode.
  • Impact: community virality for a niche game.

7. Guiding Principles for the Roadmap

Three rules for what gets built next
  1. Engine first. New mechanics land in src/engine/ with tests before any UI. The purity boundary is non-negotiable.
  2. Close open loops before opening new ones. A feature that deepens an existing system (loans → transfers → youth) beats a parallel new system at the same effort.
  3. Determinism is a feature. Anything that would break replay (e.g. real-time clocks, non-seeded randomness) is rejected or reworked to flow through the shared RNG.
